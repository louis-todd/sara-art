About Me

I graduated from Welden college with a Diploma in Integrative Humanistic Counselling in 2010. Integrative Psychotherapy takes into account many views of human fuctioning. The psychodynamic, client-centred, behviourist, cognitive, Festalt therapy and transactional analysis are all approaches considered. After graduating I worked for the Federation of Disabled People and at the Women's Centre as well as in private practice. Currently, I work for Stand Alone, a charity that supports people estranged from their families. As well as this I work with young people and the arts. I am in the second year of studying my MA in Integrative Arts Practice.

I offer different ways of exploring: sand tray, drawing, music, writing, as well as talking. Being creative and making things can help to understand feelings and experiences especially when words are hard to find. Sometimes when a difficulty has been drawn or made and is right there in front of us, it is easier to talk about. This is playful work that takes the person and their story seriously.

Currently, I have a couple of sessions available on Tuesday afternoon & evening and Thursday morning. Usually, our sessions start remotely, but if your interest is mainly around creative work we can arrange a face-to-face session. I also offer canine therapy which involves a dialogical session in the presence of my gentle therapy dog, Smilo.
